Grandma's Polish Perogies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the warm water, egg, and vegetable oil. Mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, cheddar cheese, green onions, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Use a round cutter (about 3 inches in diameter) to cut out circles from the dough.
  4. Place a spoonful of the potato filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over to create a half-moon shape and press the edges together to seal. You can use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch.
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the perogies in batches and cook until they float to the surface, about 3-4 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain.
  6. Optional: In a skillet, heat some butter and sauté the cooked perogies until they are golden brown on both sides. Serve with sour cream or your favorite toppings.
